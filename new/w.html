A common-born child, Griffith was poverty-stricken, at times unable to obtain even a slice of bread to eat.[21] In the cobblestone streets of alleyways, he played with other children, procuring junk trinkets and regarding them as small victories. Griffith's ambition proved to be higher than that of the other children, however; in the afternoons, with the sunlight blocked out by towering brothels and pubs, he would gaze up at a sun-coated castle. It was in these streets where Griffith set his mind to acquiring "that thing".[4] He eventually received his Crimson Beherit from an old, gypsy fortune teller, who foretold his conquest of the world.[22] In his adolescence, he formed the Band of the Falcon, including the miner Pippin, former bandit Corkus, and tumbler Judeau. While still collecting war funds for his nascent group, Griffith came across a distressed 12-year-old Casca struggling to free herself from the clutches of a lustful nobleman. He interrupted the confrontation and sliced the assailant's ear off, displeased with the latter's elitist nobility. Rather than kill the would-be rapist himself, he threw Casca his sword and instructed her to wield it if she had something to protect. With the nobleman's death and Casca's newfound devotion, Griffith told her she was free to do as she pleased, with the girl promptly joining his ranks afterward.[37] The Band of the Falcon's big break came when they found themselves under the employ of the wealthy Tudor feudal lord Gennon. Realizing the perverted nobleman fancied him, and that Gennon posed a faster, safer route to amassing and funding an army, Griffith sold his body to the man in exchange for a substantial fortune to give his band members a better chance of survival.[27] Story Golden Age Arc Meeting Guts Years later, the Band of the Falcon finds itself contracted with the defense of a military garrison from a series of mercenary attacks. For three months, the mercenaries are able to hold out in the fort, but with the defeat of Bazuso at the hands a young mercenary named Guts, and the subsequent blow to the garrison soldiers' morale, they relinquish control of the fort to the enemy and abandon their post. A day later, some band members catch sight of Guts walking on a trail near their encampment, a few of which decide to pursue him in hopes of taking his recently acquired reward. One after another, they fall victim to Guts' dominance. Eventually, Casca, too, finds herself at the mercy of the mercenary; with his then closest confidant in danger, Griffith finally dons his armor and arrives to the scene, intercepting Guts' attack with a spear. He attempts to reason with Guts, but to no avail, left only with the option of dismantling and incapacitating the swordsman.[6] Manga E0-14 Griffith Claims Guts Griffith defeats and lays claim to Guts. With his interest piqued, Griffith has Guts taken to the mercenaries' nearby encampment and orders Casca to lie with the injured mercenary to provide him with warmth. When Guts awakens, Griffith attempts to enlist him, an offer which Guts refuses. Guts gives Griffith a choice: if he can defeat him, he can claim ownership of the mercenary; if he loses, however, he'll be granted a wound similar to that which he dealt Guts days before. Griffith agrees to Guts' terms and engages him in combat.[24] Eventually, Griffith dislocates Guts' arm and claims the swordsman for himself as a member of his mercenary band.[38] Later on, in a night raid, Griffith orders Guts to lead the rear guard. Griffith raids the enemy forces, destroying their tents before reinforcements could arrive. After regrouping he learns from one of his Band that Guts is still dealing with the enemy causing him to ride off looking for him, as Guts' horse is killed and he is nearly attacked by the enemy Griffith, Judeau and Pippin come to his rescue as the rest of the Band members manage to drive off the enemy into a retreat. During their celebration of their victory Griffith wonders where Guts was only to spot him sitting alone away from the party, when Pippin manages to drag Guts to join in the celebration Griffith smiles.[7] The next day Guriffith calls for Guts while he is taking a shower as the two engage in a water fight. Afterwards Guts notices Griffiths necklace to which he explains it is a Beherit that he was given by an old fortune teller years ago, during their chat Griffith explains his intentions to Guts that he will one day attain his own kingdom and that Guts "belongs to him" and he alone will decide where he dies.[22] At some unspecified point later, Grifith finds Guts under a pile of enemy corpses. The White Falcon is impressed by Guts' luck of surviving as he pulls him up. Griffith then speaks about what his role in the world may be and what it means to be the "true elite" as he muses on how Guts is the first person he talked to about these things.[13] Ascension Manga E1 Viscount Griffith Griffith is knighted and made a viscount. Three years later, when the Band of the Falcon successfully defends the Midland army against the Black Ram Iron Lance heavy cavalry's attack, they are enlisted by the kingdom full-time in its century-spanning war against Tudor. As a reward for his valiant defensive efforts, Griffith is knighted by the king of Midland and granted the title of viscount, at long last obtaining a peerage among the Midland nobility and taking one step closer to realizing his dream.[1]
